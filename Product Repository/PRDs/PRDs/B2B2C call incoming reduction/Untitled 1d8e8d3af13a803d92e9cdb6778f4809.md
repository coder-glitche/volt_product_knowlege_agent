# **Banking & Mandate Setup (162)**

## Digital Mandate Challenges

- Mandate setup often fails due to client-side issues with Net Banking, Debit Card, or Aadhaar OTP methods.
- Specific banks (e.g., Cooperative, J&K) and joint account setups commonly trigger mandate failures.
- OTPs for mandate verification are frequently delayed, not received, or sent to a different mobile number than the one used in the application.
- Technical glitches like timeouts are common during the client's mandate registration attempt.
- Digital failures force partners to rely on the slower, physical mandate process.
- These issues frequently delay overall client application timelines.

**Action step:** Introduce a pre-check tool for mandate readiness and clearer guidance based on bank type and account structure.

## Bank Account Verification Issues

- Bank account verification is delayed due to system lags or document submission problems.
- Clients often upload unclear cheque images or select incorrect account types (e.g., savings vs. current).
- Verification failures lead to partners assisting clients in switching or re-submitting alternate bank details.

**Action step:** Add real-time document quality checks and auto-suggestions for account type validation.

## Name Mismatches

- Minor name differences between the client's PAN and bank records (e.g., initials, prefixes) cause mandate or verification failures.
- These mismatches require backend manual resolution and can significantly stall progress.
- Partners often have to collect and submit extra documents from clients to prove identity.

**Action step:** Implement a smart name-matching logic with a flag + resolution path for minor differences.

## IFSC & Penny Drop Failures

- Incorrect or outdated IFSC codes lead to delays during bank validation.
- Partners often help clients correct and re-enter valid IFSC codes.
- Penny drop verification either fails or takes too long, putting client applications on hold.

**Action step:** Enable IFSC auto-lookup and provide live penny drop status to reduce partner follow-up load.
