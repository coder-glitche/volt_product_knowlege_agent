# User Research Framework

### User research guide

## **Step 1 : Defining Purpose & Objectives**

Define the goals of the research to ensure alignment across stakeholders.

The research objective should answer

- What decision do you need to make for which you need data through user research?
- What assumptions are you trying to validate
- What would success of this user research look like

## **Step 2 : Target the right and varied User Segments**

To ensure comprehensive insights, target varied user groups based on relevant characteristics. Use the following parameters to define your user cohorts:

1. **By User Personas:**
    - Leverage pre-defined personas representing key demographics, behaviors, or motivations.
    - Example:
        - **Persona A**: New users exploring the product.
        - **Persona B**: Long-term users engaging with advanced features.
        
2. **By Task-Based Segmentation:**
    - Categorize users based on their interactions with specific features or tasks:
        - **Completion:** Users who successfully completed the task.
        - **Abandonment:** Users who started the task but dropped off.
        - **Non-Starters:** Users who never attempted the task.

### **3. Research Questions**

- **Purpose:** Develop open-ended questions to explore behaviors, pain points, and needs.
- **Template Examples:**
    - *What motivates you to consider leveraging your stock portfolio for a loan?*
    - *Can you walk me through the process of securing such a loan? What felt easy or difficult?*
    - *What emotions did you experience during this process?*
    - *What would make this experience more seamless or appealing?*
    - *How do you currently decide which financial product to use?*

**Validation Type: Testing Solutions for Effectiveness**

- **Navigation Ease**: Can users find what they need intuitively?
- **Information Clarity**: Are instructions and labels easy to understand?
- **Emotional Triggers**: Are there moments of frustration or delight during use?
- **Actionability of Features**: Are buttons, menus, and actions intuitive?
- **Consistency**: Does the interface align with user expectations?
- **Error Recovery**: Are error messages helpful, and can users recover quickly?
- **Accessibility**: Is the product usable for people with diverse abilities?

**Tracking Type: Monitoring User Interactions**

- Drop off screens
- Wrong clicks : Are users clicking in places where they’re not supposed to
- Misunderstanding of information
- Are wrong items being highlighted
- Are elements confusing to the understand

## Step 5: Building a script and Questionnaire & Notetaking Dump

Start by identifying what you need to learn, based on the research goals. For each parameter, consider specific aspects:

- **For User Persona Research**:
    - Understand motivations, goals, fears, and influences.
    - Example Question: *"What do you hope to achieve by using this product?"*
- **For Drop-Off Analysis**:
    - Focus on:
        - **Navigation Usability**: Can users find their way easily?
        - **Clarity of Communication**: Is the information straightforward?
        - **Technical Issues**: Are bugs or slow load times causing frustration?
        - **Behavioral Insights**: Are users experiencing *"User Anxiety"* (e.g., concerns about sharing sensitive data)? Is there trust in the process?
    - Example Question: *"What concerns, if any, do you have when filling out your details?"*

### **Structure Your Questionnaire**

### **2.1 Warm-Up Questions**

- Make the user comfortable before we ask the questions that need the user to think in depth.
- Example: *"How did you hear about volt?"*

### **2.2 Core Questions**

- Focus on your research objectives.
- Ask follow up questions for depth and specificity.
- Example:
    - For navigation: *"What, if anything, felt confusing or unclear while navigating the credit limit screen?"*
    - For usability: *"Did you face any technical issues, like slow loading or unresponsive buttons?"*

Make sure you have the questionnaire in relevant languages (e.g., Hindi, Kanada, Marathi) to cater to diverse user groups.

### **Example Breakdown**

If your goal is understanding **drop-offs from the credit limit screen**, your script might look like this:

- **Warm-Up**:
    - *"How was your overall experience with volt so far?"*
- **Core Questions**:
    - Navigation: *"Were you able to find the information you needed while on the credit limit screen easily?"*
    - Anxiety: *"Did anything about the process make you hesitant to proceed?"*

## Step 6: Analyze & Synthesize Insights

### **Frameworks for Analysis**

- **Affinity Mapping**: Organize qualitative insights into themes (e.g., trust, speed, repayment).
- **Customer Journey Mapping**: Identify moments of delight and frustration across the borrowing lifecycle.

## Step 7: Conclusion

- Write down your insights per objective
- Build action items for every objective
- Further build experiments if necessary to test the understanding
